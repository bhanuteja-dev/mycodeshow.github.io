Math Track — runs throughout all phases
30–45 min/day
▲
The rule: You don't need to be a mathematician. You need to read a paper and not panic when you see a gradient, a matrix multiply, or a KL divergence. Every resource here was selected because it tells a story — visual, intuitive, never a wall of symbols. Velleman's proof style but for applied math.
YouTubeStep 1 — Linear Algebra (weeks 1–3 of phase 1)
3Blue1Brown — Essence of Linear Algebra (YouTube, free)
16 short videos. Grant Sanderson doesn't teach you to compute — he makes you see what a matrix actually does to space. Dot products, eigenvectors, and the change-of-basis concept will never feel abstract again. You can finish this in a weekend. Do it twice. This is the best math content on the internet, period.
visual · free
Immersive Math — Linear Algebra (immersivemath.com, free)
Interactive textbook. Every concept has a manipulable visual. Read it after 3B1B to lock in intuition with a bit more rigour. Vectors, dot products, matrix operations, eigenvalues — all clickable, draggable. No dry wall of definitions.
interactive · free
YouTubeStep 2 — Calculus for ML (weeks 3–5)
3Blue1Brown — Essence of Calculus (YouTube, free)
11 videos. Derivatives and integrals told as a detective story — why did Newton need to invent this? You'll understand gradients, the chain rule, and partial derivatives as geometry, not as symbol manipulation. This is all you need for understanding backprop.
visual · free
The Matrix Calculus You Need for Deep Learning — Parr & Howard (free PDF)
Jeremy Howard (fast.ai founder) co-wrote this. 33 pages that bridge "I understand derivatives" to "I can read a backpropagation derivation." Reads like a tutor explaining, not a textbook defining. Do this before Phase 4 (deep learning).
paper · free
YouTubeStep 3 — Probability & Stats (weeks 5–8)
StatQuest with Josh Starmer — Statistics Fundamentals playlist (YouTube, free)
Josh has a gift. He explains p-values, probability distributions, Bayes' theorem, and hypothesis testing with hand-drawn visuals and zero pretension. Specific videos to cover: Normal Distribution, Probability vs Likelihood, Bayes' Theorem, MLE. Each video is under 20 minutes. Binge-able.
visual · free
StatQuest — Machine Learning playlist (YouTube, free)
This is the bridge from probability into ML. StatQuest covers cross-entropy, KL divergence, information gain, and the statistics behind every algorithm you'll use. Watch these alongside Phase 2 courses — they're the "why does this work" for every algorithm you're implementing.
visual · free
BlogStep 4 — Information Theory (before Phase 6, NLP)
Visual Information Theory — Chris Olah (colah.github.io, free)
Single blog post, maybe 20 minutes to read. Chris Olah explains entropy, cross-entropy, KL divergence and mutual information with the most beautiful visual diagrams. You'll read it once and suddenly understand why loss functions are shaped the way they are. Required before touching LLMs seriously.

Phase 8 — Reinforcement Learning: MDPs to Actor-Critic
Pluralsight + Sutton & Barto
▼
RL is increasingly relevant for three areas: RLHF (how ChatGPT is trained), recommendation systems (long-horizon reward optimisation), and agentic systems (policy learning). The Pluralsight coverage is solid. The Sutton & Barto book is the canonical reference — but it's not a light read. Use it as a reference alongside, not as a primary text.
PluralsightReinforcement Learning path — full sequence
Introduction to Reinforcement Learning
Markov Decision Processes, Dynamic Programming, Monte Carlo methods. MDPs are the formal framework that everything else in RL builds on — understand them properly.
core
Getting Started with Gymnasium
Gymnasium (OpenAI Gym successor) for designing and running simulation environments. Practical hands-on — agents need environments to learn in.
core
Introduction to Q-learning
Tabular Q-learning, Q-tables, DQN (Deep Q-Network). Q-learning is the conceptual gateway to modern deep RL. The DQN section connects Phase 4 (deep learning) with RL.
core
Monte Carlo Methods
Episodic sampling, first-visit vs every-visit estimation, ε-greedy policy control. Monte Carlo methods are conceptually clean — excellent for building intuition before policy gradients.
core
Actor-critic Methods and Advantage Estimation
A2C, A3C, SAC, advantage estimation. Actor-critic is the architecture family that underlies PPO, which is what InstructGPT uses for RLHF. Connect this explicitly to Phase 6's RLHF course.
core
Modified Reinforcement Learning
Reward shaping, inverse RL, constraint handling, hierarchical RL. Real-world RL is always constrained — this is where textbook RL meets production systems.
